Project Management Roles

  • Grassroots Founder

    Grassroots Founders are new to the technology landscape and are actively learning the ropes. These individuals are enthusiastic and eager to leverage innovative tools to bring their ideas to life. They are likely to be frequent users of AI-driven platforms like project-engine at launch, using its capabilities to streamline their learning and project execution processes.

  • Corporate Project Manager

    Corporate Project Managers lead internal teams, focusing on people management and incentive strategies to ensure projects meet technical goals and adhere to planned timelines. In the project-engine landscape, they are embodied by Sparky the Technician, a persona representing technical expertise and leadership in driving project success.

  • Corporate Programme Manager

    Corporate Programme Managers ensure that business objectives are met, keeping clients and investors satisfied. They intervene at higher levels to keep projects on track and manage external risk registers. In the project-engine literacy, they are represented by Sergeant RoboSnail, a figure symbolizing strategic oversight and resilience in navigating complex project environments.

  • Blockchain OG

    Blockchain OGs are seasoned professionals who have successfully navigated a blockchain project to launch, earning respect and standing among their peers. They may participate in Blockchain communities or Decentralized Autonomous Organizations (DAOs). A DAO is a blockchain-based organization governed by smart contracts, where decisions are made collectively by stakeholders through transparent voting mechanisms. Blockchain OGs often provide governance for these chains, contributing their expertise to ensure stability and growth.

  • Developer

    Developers are critical to project success, often working under the guidance of startup founders who have secured funding or Blockchain OG communities commissioning development work. Their work is divided into front-end and back-end roles. Front-end developers focus on the user interface, ensuring seamless and visually appealing interactions, while back-end developers handle server-side logic, databases, and application functionality to ensure robust performance.

  • Content Creator

    Content Creators share similar dependencies as Developers, relying on startup founders and Blockchain OG communities for project direction. They are responsible for crafting the user experience, ensuring a cohesive look and feel, and enabling smooth transitions between web-based screens and assets. Their work enhances user engagement and ensures a polished, professional presentation of the project.
