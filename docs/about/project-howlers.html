The 10 Most Common Killers (and How Elite PMs Actually Fix Them)

1. Project sprawl → surgical tightening & kill-the-darlings ceremonies
The project keeps growing fatter with “quick little features” until nothing ships. Elite PMs schedule recurring “kill-the-darlings” sessions where anything not in the top 20% of impact gets axed — no debate, no mercy.
2. Scope creep → 80:20 ruthless MVP + change budget with real $ tags
Every new idea sounds great until the timeline explodes. Fix: lock the MVP at the 20% of features that deliver 80% of value. Any addition after day one goes into a change budget with a real dollar cost attached and requires sponsor signature.
3. Team disputes & politics → outcome-oriented war rooms + psych safety resets
Egos and silos kill velocity faster than bugs. Elite PMs run weekly “war rooms” that are strictly outcome-focused (no blame, only solutions) and do anonymous psych-safety pulse checks — if score <8/10, immediate off-site reset.
4. Stakeholder ghosting / misaligned expectations → forced alignment workshops + public commitment boards
Stakeholders disappear then reappear with surprise demands. Countermeasure: mandatory half-day alignment workshop at kickoff + a public Notion/Trello “Commitment Board” where every promise is written, dated, and signed. Ghosting = public flag.
5. Resource bottlenecks & hero culture → cross-functional swarming + no-hero rule
One person becomes the single point of failure; everyone waits. Elite move: enforce swarming (whole team attacks the blocker together) and a written “no-hero” policy — overtime heroics trigger mandatory time-off in lieu.
6. Schedule slippage & student syndrome → critical-chain buffering + last-responsible-moment decisions
People procrastinate because “there’s still time.” Fix: remove individual task deadlines, put one big project buffer at the end, and force decisions at the last responsible moment. Parkinson’s Law and student syndrome vanish overnight.
7. Budget bleed → real-time earned-value burn-up + contingency triggers
Money disappears with nothing to show. Elite PMs run live earned-value charts in every standup and pre-define trigger points (“if burn > 110% of plan → immediate scope cut”).
8. Hidden risks & black-swan crises → mandatory pre-mortems + red-team kill criteria
Everything looks fine until it isn’t. Elite practice: day-one pre-mortem (“assume the project failed — why?”) and explicit red-team “kill criteria” (e.g., “if TVL < $10M by week 8, we sunset”).
9. Team burnout & death-march culture → energy audits + recovery sprints + hard no-work-after-6pm
Crunch becomes the norm, quality tanks, people quit. Real fix: weekly anonymous energy audit (1–10), mandatory recovery sprint when average <7, and a hard “no work after 6 pm / weekends” rule enforced by the PM personally.
10. Quality drift & endless bug whack-a-mole → DoD gatekeeping + zero-bug policy in final 20%
Tech debt piles up, launch gets delayed forever. Elite PMs enforce a ruthless Definition of Done on every ticket and declare the last 20% of the timeline “zero-bug tolerance” — nothing merges with known bugs, period.
