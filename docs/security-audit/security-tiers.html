Why lone coders win with mass platform protection

When you vibe-code with AI, push to GitHub, and ship instantly, your repo becomes a magnet. The moment you have any assets — even a few users, a clever idea, or $100 in Stripe — you attract extractive bad actors.

Self-managing servers, certs, firewalls, and AI data leaks is exhausting and dangerous. That’s why smart builders hand the boring (and scary) stuff to massive platforms — the same reason you use Gmail instead of running your own mail server.

These platforms give you enterprise-grade security at hobbyist prices because they protect millions of sites at once. Their security teams, global edge networks, and constant threat intelligence are your invisible bodyguard.
