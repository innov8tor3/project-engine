Navigating the 2026 Reforms

A guide to the April 2026 Universal Credit changes for health-limited claimants and casual work opportunities.

The Assessment Outcomes

The Three Main Routes

Following the Work Capability Assessment (WCA), the DWP places claimants into one of three distinct categories. These dictate your financial award and your work-related obligations.

Route 1

Fit for Work

No health-related top-up. Claimants are expected to search for work full-time.

Route 2

LCW

Limited Capability for Work. No extra cash award, but access to the Work Allowance and zero pressure to search for work immediately.

Route 3

LCWRA

The Health Element. Divided into Higher and Lower tiers (£429.80 or £217.26 top-ups). No mandatory work activities.

Earning while Claiming

The "Casual Work" Advantage

The 2026 legislation significantly protects those who can work "odd days" or perform one-off tasks. Unlike previous systems, there is no "permitted work" hour limit.

Scenario: You earn £100 from a one-off job.

If you have the LCW/LCWRA status, this falls under your monthly Work Allowance.

  • The Work Allowance: A shield that lets you earn up to £404 (with housing) or £673 (without housing) before the 55% taper starts.
  • The Taper Rate: Earnings above your allowance are only reduced by 55p per £1, ensuring you are always better off working.
Why 2026 is Different

The "Right to Try" Guarantee

One of the most significant improvements in the recent legislation is the reduction of fear. The new framework moves away from sanctions and toward "trial" opportunities.

Security Guarantee: Under the 2026 reforms, starting a casual job or accepting a short contract **cannot** be the sole trigger for a medical reassessment. You have a legal "Right to Try" work without losing your LCWRA status if the work proves unsustainable for your health.

Voluntary Support

Advisors now offer WorkWell and Connect to Work schemes. Crucially, these are 100% voluntary for the health group. You can opt-in for help without any risk of sanctions if you decide to stop.

Simplified Reporting

Casual earnings are tracked via HMRC. If you do a one-off job, you simply report the income in your journal at the end of the month. No complex forms or prior permission required.
