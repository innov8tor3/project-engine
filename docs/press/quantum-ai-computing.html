Key Integration Projects

Leibniz Supercomputing Centre (LRZ) with IQM, Germany:

A 20-qubit superconducting quantum computer from IQM was deployed into the SuperMUC-NG supercomputer in early 2025. It has operated autonomously for over 100 days, supporting hybrid quantum-classical algorithms for optimization and simulation.

Oak Ridge National Laboratory (ORNL) with NVIDIA and HPE, USA:

Advancing a quantum-AI-HPC platform with installation set for early 2026, incorporating Blackwell GPUs and CUDA-Q software for AI-driven simulations in energy and climate modeling.

RIKEN with NVIDIA, Japan:

Deploying two new supercomputers in spring 2026 focused on AI for science and quantum computing, delivering up to 100x performance gains.

ParTec AG and ORCA Computing, Europe:

Integrating room-temperature photonic quantum computers into AI Factory supercomputers for energy-efficient processing in industries like healthcare and aerospace.

LUMI Supercomputer with EuroHPC Initiatives, Europe:

Initiating quantum AI projects since mid-2025, with hybrid HPC+AI+QC setups for European users in academia and industry.

Quantinuum and NVIDIA, USA:

Collaborating since March 2025 on hybrid workflows for Generative Quantum AI, improving error correction for applications in drug discovery and finance.

Conclusion

These projects highlight a growing trend toward hybrid ecosystems, with rapid scaling expected. This discussion underscores the potential for breakthroughs in computational power through integration of quantum computing, supercomputers, and AI.
