Lessons from the DotCom era + today's acceleration factors
Updated November 2025

Your analogy to the DotCom era is spot-on — hype-driven overinvestment, overstated business cases, and a high dropout rate among smaller players are recurring themes in transformative tech waves.

Current projections suggest 90–99% of AI startups could fail by 2026–2027, driven by poor retention, lack of true innovation, and failure to deliver ROI. At the lower levels, the investor estimate of a 1% success rate feels almost optimistic in this fevered environment.

We're not in a pure DotCom redux. We have the learnings from e-commerce (profitability > growth at all costs, customer value first, lean operations), existing cloud infrastructure, and AI's self-reinforcing nature that compresses timelines dramatically. Global enterprise adoption is already at ~75%, far ahead of where internet penetration was at the 2000 peak.
